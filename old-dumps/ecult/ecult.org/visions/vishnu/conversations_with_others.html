Conversations with Others

Publication date: Feb 27, 2006 12:26:21 AM

A big part of what I want to do is improve the way people communicate.  I'd like to see the trail left by spontaneous conversation accrete into something that is useful to others.  I'd like to see the usefulness of information improve as people use it.  This is actually a feature of many traditional forms of human communication, but it is especially easy to end up with a disorganized mess in the world of e-conversations.

A big part of what I want to do is combine insights from science and holistic approaches to the improvement of movement.  Example: Lucy Brown published a paper about how the basal ganglia are structured with nodes and meridians - just like in Traditional Chinese Medicine.  Maybe TCM has stumbled on to these very real patterns in our nervous systems, only to misclassify them.  But we can hardly blame the Chinese.  And if it weren't for them, it's likely Lucy wouldn't have looked for that kind of organization.

So I'm starting a page for conversations.  Sure, right now it's klunky.  But I'm still learning.  Hopefully it's good enough for now, and things will continue to get better.
